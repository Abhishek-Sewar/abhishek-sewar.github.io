My path into AI started with Mechatronics, a discipline that forced me to think about perception, control loops, and real-world robustness before I ever wrote a neural network. When a system has to actuate in the physical world, "good enough on the benchmark" doesn't cut it. That engineering mindset is the lens I bring to every ML problem I touch.

At Northeastern, I'm deepening that foundation by studying Transformers, Robot Perception, Cloud ML, and Responsible AI. But I'm not waiting to apply it. I build LLM pipelines that ship, computer vision systems trained to perform under variable lighting and occlusion, and robotics solutions grounded in the mathematics of state estimation. These aren't notebook experiments; they're systems designed to survive contact with production data.

What drives me is the gap between research and deployment. I care about clean architecture, responsible design, and closing that gap: building AI that is explainable, measurable, and useful to the humans on the other side of it. If you're working on problems that sit at that frontier, I want to be in that conversation.
